Preparing for USMLE Step 1 is one of the most significant challenges in medical school. With hundreds of resources available, it's easy to fall into the trap of over-studying the wrong materials. This guide cuts through the noise and tells you exactly what to use — and what to skip.

## The Core Trio: First Aid, UWorld, and Anki

Every successful Step 1 study plan starts with three pillars.

### First Aid for the USMLE Step 1

First Aid is the non-negotiable foundation. Published annually, it distills high-yield pathophysiology, pharmacology, microbiology, and biochemistry into a format that mirrors the actual exam. The 2024 edition includes updated content reflecting recent guideline changes.

**How to use it:** Don't read First Aid passively. Annotate as you go through other resources. Use it as an anchor — not a primary teacher.

**Verdict:** Essential. Buy the current edition.

### UWorld Step 1 Qbank

UWorld remains the gold standard question bank for Step 1. Its explanations are detailed enough to function as a standalone resource, and the question style closely mirrors the actual exam.

- Over 3,000 USMLE-style questions
- Detailed explanations with diagrams
- Performance analytics to identify weak areas

**Tip:** Start doing UWorld in tutor mode early. Switch to timed mode in your final 4–6 weeks.

### Anki (with a premade deck)

Anki's spaced repetition algorithm is one of the most evidence-based study methods available. Rather than building your own deck from scratch, use the **Anking Overhaul** deck — a community-maintained masterpiece that covers all of First Aid.

Start your Anki routine on Day 1 and never skip a review session. Students who maintain consistent Anki habits consistently outperform those who abandon it.

## Visual Learning: Sketchy and Pathoma

### Sketchy Medical

Sketchy uses visual storytelling scenes to teach pharmacology and microbiology. If you're a visual learner, Sketchy will feel like a cheat code. A single 20-minute Sketchy video can cement 30 drug mechanisms in a way that traditional memorization never could.

**Best for:** Micro (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites) and Pharm

### Pathoma

Dr. Sattar's Pathoma is the most beloved pathology resource in medical education. The concise videos and accompanying textbook break down complex pathology into digestible, high-yield content. Most students finish all 13 chapters 2–3 times before their dedicated study period ends.

**How to use it:** Watch Pathoma alongside the corresponding First Aid chapter.

---

## Boards and Beyond: The Physiology Deep Dive

For students who need stronger foundational understanding in physiology and pharmacology, Boards and Beyond by Dr. Ryan fills gaps that Pathoma doesn't cover. The videos are calm, methodical, and excellent for building frameworks.

Many students use Boards and Beyond during their pre-clinical years and return to it during dedicated as a review.

---

## What to Skip (or Deprioritize)

- **Robbins Pathology textbook** — Too detailed for Step 1 purposes. Use Pathoma instead.
- **Kaplan Qbank** — Lower yield than UWorld. Use it only if you've exhausted UWorld questions.
- **Lecturio / Osmosis** — Decent supplementary tools but not worth the cost if you already have Sketchy and Boards & Beyond.

## Building Your Study Schedule

A typical 8–10 week dedicated Step 1 schedule looks like this:

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1–2 | Biochemistry + Genetics (First Aid + B&B) |
| 3–4 | Microbiology + Immunology (Sketchy + First Aid) |
| 5–6 | Pathology (Pathoma + First Aid) |
| 7–8 | Pharmacology (Sketchy Pharm + First Aid) |
| 9 | UWorld second pass + weak areas |
| 10 | NBME practice exams + review |

Do 40–60 UWorld questions daily throughout, and maintain your Anki deck every single day.

## Practice Exams and Timing Your Test Date

NBME free practice exams are your most accurate predictor of your actual score. Use them to gauge readiness — not as a study tool. Take your first NBME at the start of dedicated to set a baseline, then again every 2 weeks.

**Score predictors in order of reliability:**
1. NBME practice exams (official)
2. UWorld Self-Assessment
3. Amboss Self-Assessment

Set your test date when you're consistently scoring 10–15 points above your target score.

---

## Final Thoughts

The best Step 1 resources are the ones you actually use consistently. Resist the urge to add more resources. Master fewer things deeply. Students who score above 250 are rarely using more than 3–4 resources — they've just gone deeper into them.

Stick to First Aid, UWorld, Anki (Anking), and either Pathoma or Sketchy depending on your learning style. That's it.
